About

Translating & editing written Italian content for anglophone audiences as part of traineeship at Milan-based start-up.

I maintained speed & accuracy, worked to tight deadlines & always kept the reading experience of each finished product in mind.

Result

I delivered 21 personal assignments over 4 months to professional translation times & standards as a trainee translator.

Challenges

Every translation has a different audience

Content varies in type & register

Creating content appropriate to specific target culture(s)

Working to tight deadlines & ad hoc

Matching schedules with editor(s) to collaborate on final product

“as an innate part of the translation process, translators build a mental model of what the new target audience may be like: what their motivation is to read the text, what they will use the text for, how much they already know about the subject matter, and so on”

— A. Ketola & R. Oittinen

Process

1. Understand client objectives & motives for translation

2. Understand content

3. Identify challenges

4. Independently brainstorm possible solutions

5. Research new vocabulary & cultural elements (online, dictionaries, native speakers)

6. Access or create terminology sheet for brand consistency

7. Draft

8. Re-draft

9. Collaborate with editor(s) to create final product & accept changes or suggest alternative solutions

10. Finalise draft

11. Quality control checks

12. Deliver to client
