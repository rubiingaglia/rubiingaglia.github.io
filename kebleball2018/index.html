Keble Ball 2018

Goal: Optimise guest experience

Role: Logistics Coordinator*

*executive role on extra-curricular committee attained through written application & panel interview

Transferable skills gained

Designing enjoyable & memorable experiences for many individuals

Communicating & collaborating with a diverse range of people

Conquering challenges with a budget, deadlines & plenty of red tape

About

Annual 2000-guest event hosted on 150-year-old, grade one listed site.

I improved on the 2017 ball by finding solutions that prioritised guest experience.

Result

Highly successful ball that satisfied the host (college), guests (students, parents, alumni, tutors, friends, family) & team (21 members)

Reported improvement on entrance process: new to 2018 ticket-scanning software

Large-scale project achieved within budget

Feedback survey sent to guests as starting point for 2019 ball preparations

The event ran without a hitch which is a testament to Rubi's organisational skills and attention to detail.
— Benjamin Jacob, Keble Ball President

Challenges

3-hour window when college guests must evacuate college grounds for ball set-up

Strict rules imposed by host-venue for use of grounds e.g. largest outdoor space largely off-limits, ruling out bigger name musical acts

Set-up time limited by graduation event running in tandem with set-up

Process

Independent research

1. Interviewed 2017 ball guests to ask what they would change: recurrent complaints were queueing, lack of seating & the quality of the silent disco DJs

2. Took oversights & misplaced priorities from previous ball and chose where our energies & efforts would be best focused in order to make a noticeable change & impact

3. Organised logistics of event & supported sub-committees & ball presidents when needed

Collaboration

1. Organised sub-committee meetings to brainstorm big ideas then narrowed down to what was achievable within budget & with time/space restrictions

2. Delegated tasks with regular progress updates in person or via Slack/email

3. Attempted collaboration with nearby colleges for my new to 2018 'pre-ball’ idea: fill 3-hour window when college students must evacuate college grounds for ball set-up by agreeing they can visit nearby college bars, giving guests a place in the interim, with discounted drinks, not too far from the host-venue. Unfortunately, the host-venue rejected this type of inter-college collaboration without justification

4. Worked with the host-venue, team & external suppliers for 8 months to create the best possible guest experience

Solutions

Enjoyable queuing experience

1. Used new to 2018 barriers to direct queuing to each vendor & separate audience from queues, as opposed to disorderly clumps of people (2017)

2. Welcome drink offered & multiple photographers strategically stationed along queue route

3. For most impactful entrance to event, suggestion of queueing outside college but this was deemed inappropriate for health & safety reasons

Seamless entrance & ticketing experience

After experiencing slow ticketing in 2017, I collaborated with the IT sub-committee who implemented new to 2018 ticket scanning software using committee members’ mobile phones, which:

1. Expedited guests’ entrance into college and ball

2. Maximised guests' positive first impression of the event

3. Achieved positive guest feedback: quickest and most efficient ball entry

Intuitive layout & clear signage

1. Re-designed map after much independent brainstorming, collaboration & iteration (see below)

2. Split college into new to 2018 ‘menu bubbles’ i.e. hosted a selection of main, side, dessert & drink vendors in each section of the college

3. New to 2018 LED neon-effect signs to highlight attractions to guests & direct them to areas of the college that went unnoticed in previous years

Increased seating

1. Increased from 57% (2017) use of space suitable and/or available for seating to 100% (2018)

2. Expanded variety of seating in line with design aims of each space

3. Created new to 2018 relaxation zone, which I considered integral to an 8-hour event

Interactive experience

1. New to 2018 arcade room open for duration of event
2017: casino, a less accessible form of entertainment & open only for the first half of the event

2. Seating accompanied by visual attraction
e.g. new to 2018 bean bags for viewing cartoons & planetarium; bar seating next to water feature; seating available next to food & drink vendors

3. New to 2018 photo cubes, as well as photographers, to create more memorable experiences & another means of documenting them

4. Snapchat filter created for event
